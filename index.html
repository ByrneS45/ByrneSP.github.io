Sean Byrne

PhD Candidate, Experimental Nuclear Physics · UMass Lowell · Gamma-ray spectroscopy · Nuclear astrophysics

I’m a PhD candidate in Experimental Nuclear Physics at the University of Massachusetts Lowell in Dr. Andrew Rogers’ group. My work uses gamma-ray spectroscopy and coincidence techniques to extract nuclear structure information and constrain astrophysical reaction rates relevant to nucleosynthesis (including supernova-relevant temperatures).

I build analysis workflows in C++ (ROOT) and Python for detector calibration, peak fitting, γ–γ coincidence spectra, and uncertainty propagation. I also develop numerical tools to calculate non-resonant and resonant thermonuclear reaction rates (narrow resonance and broader contributions), including penetrability and width calculations.
