üåü Explosive Nucleosynthesis, Supernovae, and the Origin of the Elements

Explosive nucleosynthesis is the rapid production of new atomic nuclei during extreme astrophysical events. Unlike the slow fusion processes that power stars for millions of years, explosive nucleosynthesis can occur in seconds under enormous temperatures (billions of Kelvin) and densities.
