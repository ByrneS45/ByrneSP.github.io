Neutron capture & heavy elements

The r-process proceeds far from stability under extreme neutron richness, then Œ≤-decays back toward stability‚Äî generating much of the heavy-element inventory.

Proton-rich pathways: p, rp, and ŒΩp

p-process (Œ≥-process): produces certain proton-rich stable isotopes (‚Äúp-nuclei‚Äù) mainly through photodisintegration reactions ((Œ≥,n), (Œ≥,p), (Œ≥,Œ±)) on pre-existing heavy seeds in hot, explosive layers.

rp-process: rapid proton captures and Œ≤+ decays in hot, H-rich environments (classically X-ray bursts), pushing material along the proton-rich side until ‚Äúwaiting points‚Äù slow the flow.

ŒΩp-process: in proton-rich supernova outflows, neutrino interactions supply a small neutron density that enables (n,p) reactions to bypass rp waiting points, extending synthesis to heavier nuclei.

Neutron-capture pathways: s, i, and r

s-process: ‚Äúslow‚Äù neutron captures (relative to Œ≤-decay) in quiescent stellar burning; the path stays near the valley of stability.

i-process: ‚Äúintermediate‚Äù neutron flux between s and r, producing distinctive abundance patterns that can explain some chemically peculiar stars.

r-process: ‚Äúrapid‚Äù neutron captures in very neutron-rich conditions, driving far from stability and then Œ≤-decaying back.
