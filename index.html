Explosive nucleosynthesis is the rapid production of new atomic nuclei during extreme astrophysical events. When temperatures reach billions of Kelvin, reaction timescales collapse from millions of years to seconds. Material synthesized in these events is ejected into the interstellar medium and becomes part of later generations of stars and planets.
