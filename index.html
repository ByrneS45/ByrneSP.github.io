About

I’m Sean Byrne, a PhD candidate in Experimental Nuclear Physics at the University of Massachusetts Lowell, working in Dr. Andrew Rogers’ group. My research focuses on gamma-ray spectroscopy and nuclear structure measurements that inform reaction rates in astrophysical environments, with a particular interest in nucleosynthesis and supernova-relevant temperatures.

I develop analysis workflows in C++ (ROOT) and Python for high-resolution γ-ray spectroscopy, including peak fitting, coincidence analysis, detector calibration, and uncertainty propagation. I also use numerical tools to calculate non-resonant and resonant thermonuclear reaction rates, incorporating penetrability, partial widths, and narrow/broad resonance formalisms.

Before graduate school I served in the United States Marine Corps, and I bring that same discipline and mission focus to research, collaboration, and technical problem solving.

Research Interests

  • Experimental nuclear physics & gamma-ray spectroscopy
  • Nuclear astrophysics: resonance structure and reaction-rate constraints
  • Detector calibration, coincidence methods, and background suppression
  • Computational analysis in C++/ROOT and Python

Current Work

Recently, I’ve been modeling thermonuclear (p,γ) reaction rates for supernova-relevant temperatures, combining direct-capture and resonance contributions, and comparing how structure inputs (e.g., spectroscopic factors) propagate into rate uncertainties.
